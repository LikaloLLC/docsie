NASSCOM

Master this essential documentation concept

Quick Definition

NASSCOM (National Association of Software and Service Companies) is India's premier trade association for the IT and BPM industries, serving as a catalyst for industry growth and development. It provides standards, guidelines, and best practices for technology documentation, while offering networking opportunities, professional development resources, and advocacy for documentation professionals working in the technology sector.

Understanding NASSCOM

NASSCOM, the National Association of Software and Service Companies, was established in 1988 as a non-profit organization to facilitate business and trade in software and services and to encourage advancement of research in software technology. Today, it represents over 3,000 member companies that constitute approximately 90% of the industry's revenue and has become the voice of India's $227 billion technology industry.

Key Features

  • Industry Standards: NASSCOM develops and promotes documentation standards and best practices specific to the Indian IT sector
  • Knowledge Repository: Maintains extensive resources, white papers, and case studies on technical documentation approaches
  • Certification Programs: Offers specialized certifications for documentation professionals to validate skills and knowledge
  • Special Interest Groups (SIGs): Hosts dedicated groups for technical writers, content strategists, and documentation managers
  • Industry Research: Publishes reports on documentation trends, tools, and methodologies relevant to the technology sector
  • Networking Platform: Facilitates connections between documentation professionals across member organizations

Benefits for Documentation Teams

  • Professional Development: Access to workshops, webinars, and training programs specifically for documentation professionals
  • Industry Recognition: Opportunities to participate in documentation excellence awards and showcases
  • Benchmarking: Access to industry standards to measure documentation quality and processes
  • Career Advancement: Job boards, mentorship programs, and career development resources
  • Global Exposure: Connections to international documentation practices and standards
  • Advocacy: Representation for documentation-specific concerns in technology policy discussions

Common Misconceptions

  • NASSCOM is only for software developers: While it began with a focus on software, NASSCOM has dedicated resources and initiatives for all technology professionals, including documentation specialists
  • Membership is only for large corporations: NASSCOM offers tiered membership options suitable for organizations of all sizes, including startups and individual professionals
  • NASSCOM is only relevant in India: With global partnerships and international standards alignment, NASSCOM's resources are valuable for documentation teams worldwide
  • It's purely a networking organization: Beyond networking, NASSCOM provides concrete tools, standards, and resources that directly improve documentation practices
