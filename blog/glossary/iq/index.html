IQ

Master this essential documentation concept

Quick Definition

Installation Qualification (IQ) is a validation protocol that verifies equipment is installed correctly according to design specifications before operational use. It serves as the first phase of equipment validation, ensuring all components are properly installed, configured, and documented. IQ provides the foundation for subsequent operational and performance qualification phases.

Understanding IQ

Installation Qualification (IQ) represents the critical first step in equipment validation, establishing that systems are installed correctly and meet predetermined design specifications. This systematic verification process ensures that all equipment components, utilities, and environmental conditions align with approved requirements before moving to operational testing phases.

Key Features

  • Systematic verification of equipment installation against approved specifications
  • Documentation of all installed components, utilities, and connections
  • Confirmation of proper environmental conditions and safety systems
  • Verification of calibration certificates and maintenance schedules
  • Review of installation drawings, manuals, and standard operating procedures
  • Testing of basic functionality and safety interlocks

Benefits for Documentation Teams

  • Establishes standardized templates and checklists for consistent validation documentation
  • Creates comprehensive equipment records that support regulatory compliance
  • Provides clear audit trails linking installation to design specifications
  • Reduces documentation errors through systematic verification processes
  • Enables efficient knowledge transfer between project phases and team members
  • Supports risk management through thorough documentation of critical systems

Common Misconceptions

  • IQ is not just a checkbox exercise but requires thorough technical verification
  • Documentation quality is as important as the physical installation itself
  • IQ cannot be rushed or abbreviated without compromising validation integrity
  • Successful IQ completion doesn't guarantee operational readiness without OQ and PQ
